## Suggested Corrections

**Needs Statement conventions (formula-level)**
Location: Key Concepts → Customer Need Analysis → Needs Statements (line 96) and Key Terms and Definitions Glossary → Needs Statement (line 215)
Current: "written in 'what' not 'how,' specific, positive (not negative), in active voice, using 'can' rather than 'must/should,' neutral, and understandable"
Correction: "written in 'what' not 'how,' specific, positive (not negative), an attribute of the product (not a user action), using 'can' rather than 'must/should'" — the source (lec-02 and lec-03) lists exactly five guidelines: (1) What not How, (2) Specificity, (3) Positive not Negative, (4) An Attribute of the Product, (5) Avoid Must/Should. The note substitutes "in active voice" and "neutral, and understandable" for the fourth guideline ("An Attribute of the Product"), which is a materially different requirement.
Source: lec-02 source (Needs Statements: Guidelines section); lec-03 source (Customer Needs Translation → Customer Needs Statements, line 39)

**Largest empirical source of ideas (Claim 1)**
Location: Frameworks & Mental Models → Opportunity Sourcing Heuristics (line 145)
Current: "The largest source of ideas empirically is the intersection of internally generated opportunities and customer-driven opportunities."
Correction: "The largest sources of ideas empirically are internally generated opportunities (46%) and customer-driven opportunities (23%) — together accounting for the majority of new product ideas." The word "intersection" implies a Venn-diagram overlap; the source reports two distinct, separately counted categories that together represent the highest combined share.
Source: lec-02 source (Sources of Opportunities → Push vs. Pull section, Terwiesch and Ulrich survey of 524 managers)
